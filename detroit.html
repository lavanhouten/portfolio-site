Introduction

     In order to analyze the crime rates and how they differ from place to place throughout the city of Detroit in 2021, first we needed to stratify the data by our two subdivisions of the city: zip code and neighborhood. This was done by counting the number of crimes by the given zip code value and neighborhood value for each crime. Then we displayed these values on a map, with red representing high crime and green representing low. In order to see if more robberies occurred near grocery stores specifically, we used a dataset of all the locations of grocery stores in the city and used st_buffer to find all of the crimes that occurred within a 0.5 mile radius and within a 1 mile radius. This allowed us to analyze the crime rate around grocery stores specifically.

Crime Location Findings

     From the beginning, it was defined that high rates of crime consisted of more than 1200 crimes in a neighborhood in 2021 and more than 5000 crimes in a given zip code. Low rates of crime consisted of less than 100 crimes in a given neighborhood and less than 100 crimes in a zip code. Using these parameters as guidance, it was found that there were certain areas of the city that had distinctly higher rates of crime by these standards, specifically the far eastern and far western portions of the city. Zip codes like 48228 and 48224 had the highest rates of crime throughout the city. The neighborhoods with the lowest rates of crime were generally located in the south and in a ring around the center of the city. Examples of neighborhoods located in those areas were Belle Isle and Carbon Works. These findings indicate a difference in police coverage and potentially economic welfare. It is known locally that the outer reaches of Detroit are the most dangerous and are also the most impoverished, which is supported by this data.

Grocery Store Robbery Findings

     When analyzing whether or not more robberies occurred near grocery stores, we created buffers around each grocery store in Detroit and mapped them. These buffers had radii of 0.5 and 1 mile. This enabled me to count the number of robberies that occurred nearby to grocery stores at two different levels and compare them.

     The area of a circle with a radius half the size of another circle is 1/4th the size of the larger circle. Thus, if the presence of the grocery store is indicative of more opportunities for robbery, the mean amount of robberies in the 0.5 radius buffer should be larger than ¼ th the mean amount of robberies in the larger radius. The mean for the 0.5 mile circle is 9.622 and the 1 mile circle is 29.81. This indicates that the amount of robberies that occur closer to grocery stores is higher than the amount of robberies that occur elsewhere. As expected, there is a higher spread in the IQR and standard deviation of the number of crimes that occur in a 1 mile radius when compared to the 0.5 mile radius, as there is higher variance in the potential areas within such a larger radius circle. All this makes sense as grocery stores are obvious targets for robberies as they offer a large variety of essential goods, offering things that desperate people would be willing to rob in order to survive. Thus, the observation that more robberies occur in their areas makes sense.

Conclusions

     To create this report, I manipulated geospatial data on crimes, neighborhoods, zip codes, and grocery stores in Detroit, Michigan in order to investigate the location and nature of crimes within the city. Using neighborhood and zip code data associated with each crime, I was able to discover which zip codes and neighborhoods were the most crime ridden and which were the safest. The most dangerous zip codes were located on the edges of the city whereas the safest zip codes were located in a ring around the center of the city. Using the location of robberies throughout the city and the location of grocery stores, I was able to investigate whether or not robberies were more likely to occur in areas where grocery stores were located. There were more robberies in the areas where grocery stores were. I noticed that the data for the locations of crimes could be derived from the location of each crime given by coordinates and by the given zip code and neighborhood associated with each crime. This could potentially cause some variance in the interpretation of the data if these don’t line up, but I used the given zip code and neighborhood associated with each crime.
